Why I Like It

The main reasons why I like this visualization are: it is very visually pleasing and well designed; and the subject matter is very interesting to me.

In terms of the design, there is a lot of complexity intrinsic to this data which is very well-managed by the visualization. There are 5 details that the designer is attempting to highlight which are found in the legend at the top of the visualization. Aside from the details, the main points emphasized are the path length and the quantity of trackers. By making the large-scale features and other details fade into the background through low-contrast color choices, the emphasized details can be easily viewed. However, the designer importantly still visualizes the data which is not the point of focus. This helps to emphasize the large-scale features.

For the subject matter, there are a few points that intrigue me. First, it seems there is a corrolation between the type of site and the number of trackers. Large online publications heavily track their users with trackers from a variety of providers. In addition to this, the ID is being shared between these large sites. This, in addition to the potential collection of Personally-Identifiable Information (PII) is worrisome from a privacy standpoint. There are an incredible amount of trackers on the news sites and it is kind of dissapointing how journalism has widely been polluted by the motivation for ad revenue.
